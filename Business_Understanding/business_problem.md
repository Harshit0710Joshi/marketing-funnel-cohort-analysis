# Marketing Funnel & Cohort Analysis

## 1. Business Problem
The business is experiencing low conversion rates across its marketing funnel and lacks visibility into where users drop off. Additionally, the company wants to understand how well users are retained over time after signup.

## 2. Business Context
The dataset represents an e-commerce or digital product company where users go through multiple stages such as visiting the website, signing up, adding items to cart, and completing a purchase. Improving funnel efficiency and user retention is critical for sustainable growth.

## 3. Problem Statement
The objective of this analysis is to evaluate funnel conversion rates and cohort-based user retention in order to identify drop-off points and improve overall conversion and customer lifetime value.

## 4. Business Objectives
- Analyze stage-wise funnel conversion rates
- Identify stages with the highest drop-off
- Measure user retention using cohort analysis
- Compare cohort performance over time
- Provide actionable insights to improve conversions and retention

## 5. Key Business Questions
- At which stage do users drop off the most?
- What is the conversion rate at each funnel stage?
- How well are users retained after signup?
- Which user cohorts perform better over time?
- What actions can improve funnel efficiency?

## 6. Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
- Total Users
- Funnel Conversion Rate (%)
- Stage-wise Drop-off Rate (%)
- Retention Rate (%)
- Average Revenue per User (ARPU)

## 7. Scope of Analysis
**Included:**
- User funnel events
- Signup and purchase behavior
- Retention trends by cohort

**Excluded:**
- Marketing channel attribution
- Campaign ROI analysis

## 8. Assumptions
- Funnel stages follow a defined sequence
- User events are accurately tracked
- Signup date defines cohort grouping
- Dataset reflects real user behavior

## 9. Expected Outcome
The analysis will identify funnel drop-off points and retention patterns, enabling data-driven improvements to marketing and product strategies.
